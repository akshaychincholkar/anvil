# Bucket List — Help

*Screenshot folder: `docs/help/screenshots/bucket-list/` — see numbering convention in [README.md](README.md).*

## What it is

The Bucket List is a wishlist with a price tag on it. Every wish you add has a
name and a cost — not a vague "someday I'd like to," but a number you can
actually work toward, funded straight out of the reward money you earn
elsewhere in Anvil.

## How to use it

1. **Add a wish.** Click **New wish** and give it a name (required), a cost,
   and optionally a link (to the exact product/experience) and a note.
   ![Add a wish](../../frontend/public/help/bucket-list/01-new-wish.png)
2. **Reorder by priority.** There's no category or tag — priority is purely
   ordinal. Use the up/down arrows on each item to rank what you want most at
   the top.
3. **Mark it fulfilled.** Tap the **Fulfilled** pill once you've actually
   gotten or done the thing — it stamps today's date ("Fulfilled · [date]")
   and moves it out of your active wishlist. Tap **Undo** if you tapped it by
   mistake.
   ![Fulfilled wish](../../frontend/public/help/bucket-list/02-fulfilled.png)
4. **Or let Rewards pay for it.** Over on the **Rewards** screen, flip on
   **Fulfil from bucket list**, then when you "Take" your pending reward
   money, pick a wish instead of a free amount — Anvil deducts its exact cost
   and marks it fulfilled automatically.
5. **Watch the totals.** The header shows Total wishes, Fulfilled count, and
   "Still wished" — the sum of everything you haven't gotten yet, in real
   currency.

## Why it matters

Most wishlists die of vagueness — "I want to travel more," "I'd love a good
camera someday" — floating goals with no path from here to there. Anvil's
Bucket List is deliberately boring and concrete about one thing: **a price**.

- **A number turns a wish into a target.** "Someday" has no finish line;
  "₹45,000, currently ₹12,000 earned toward it" does. Attaching a real cost
  is what makes a wish plannable instead of just felt.
- **Funding it from earned reward money closes the loop.** The Rewards screen
  converts your actual focused hours and disciplined app usage into money;
  spending that money on a wish means the things you want are being paid for
  by the version of you that's already showing up and doing the work — not by
  wishful thinking.
- **Ordinal ranking, not categories, forces an honest choice.** Most wishlist
  apps let everything sit as equally important, which is another way of
  saying nothing is prioritized. Ranking wishes against each other makes you
  decide, even loosely, what actually matters most right now.

Used consistently, the Bucket List is meant to convert "wanting things" from
a source of low-grade dissatisfaction into a visible progress bar — proof
that the effort you're putting in elsewhere in your life is actually buying
you something you chose on purpose.
